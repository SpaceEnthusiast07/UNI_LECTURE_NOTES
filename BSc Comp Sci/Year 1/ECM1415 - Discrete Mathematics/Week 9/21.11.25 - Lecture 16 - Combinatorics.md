#### Permutations and Combinations
##### Permutations
 - Let $P(n, k)$ be the *number of ways to select* $k$ items from $n$, where $1 \leq k \leq n$, *without replacement*. Then:
$$P(n,k) = n(n-1)(n-2)\dots(n-(k+1)) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}$$
 - E.g., if you have $n$ different colour pens, how many combinations of $k$ pens can you have?

 - For permutations, *order matters* $\to (1,2,3) \neq (3,2,1)$

##### Combinations
 - In combinations, *order doesn't matter*
$$\{1,2,3\} \equiv \{3,2,1\} \equiv \{2,1,3\} \equiv \dots$$
 - ![[Pasted image 20251121095121.png]]
 - Let $C(n,k)$ be the number of ways to select $k$ items from $n$ without replacement, where $1 \leq k \leq n$ and the order of the items is irrelevant
$$C(n,k) = \frac{P(n,k)}{k!} = \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)\dots(n-(k+1))}{1*2*3\dots k} = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!k!}$$
 - Proof: ![[Pasted image 20251121095456.png]]

##### Combinations with Replacement
- The number of ways to select $k$ items with replacement from a set of $n$, where the order is irrelevant, is:
$$C(n+(k-1),k)$$

##### Indistinguishable Items
 - Example: ![[Pasted image 20251121100522.png]]
 - If there are $n_{1}$ indistinguishable objects of types 1, $n_{2}$ of type and so on up to $n_{k}$, with $n = n_{1} + n_{2} + \dots + n_{k}$, then the number of permutations of these objects is:
$$\frac{n!}{n_{1}!n_{2}!\dots n_{k}!}$$
 - The result is always an integer
